Divyesh Mistry

Divyesh Mistry is a Ph.D. scholar in the Department of Aerospace Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay. His research focuses on multiscale modeling of nickel-based superalloys, addressing critical challenges in dislocation dynamics and the role of prior particle boundaries in material behavior. Divyesh employs advanced computational tools such as LAMMPS, OVITO, and high-performance computing frameworks to develop innovative models that enhance the understanding of high-strength materials used in aerospace applications. His work bridges the gap between theoretical modeling and real-world material design, contributing to performance, durability, and reliability advancements. He has led multiple high-impact projects, including developing atomistic-informed models for superalloys, investigating dislocation avalanche mechanisms, and designing parallelized molecular dynamics simulations. Proficient in Python, C++, and scientific computing, he integrates technical expertise with a strong research vision to address emerging materials science and engineering challenges.

Before his doctoral research, Divyesh Mistry completed his M.Tech. in Mechanical Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur, specializing in Mechanical System Design. His master's research involved developing a mesh-free model for the static analysis of smart composite beams. Divyesh has demonstrated academic excellence throughout his career, achieving top grades in continuum mechanics, fracture mechanics, and computational modeling. He has also actively contributed to the academic community by mentoring students, conducting workshops, and delivering lectures on advanced topics, including finite element methods and high-performance scientific computing. Divyesh Mistry's dedication to education and research underscores his commitment to fostering innovation and solving real-world challenges in aerospace engineering.

Research Projects

Multiscale Modeling Of Prior Particle Boundaries in Nickel-based Superalloys

Developed a multiscale material model to study dislocation behavior, stress distribution, and strengthening mechanisms around PPBs and γ/γ' phases. Addressed challenges in modeling complex interactions between PPBs and dislocation dynamics in Ni-based superalloys

Learn more
Dislocation Avalanche Mechanisms in Metallic Materials

Dislocation Dynamics (DDD) framework, implemented in FORTRAN, to explore the intricate mechanisms of dislocation avalanche dynamics and their influence on material behavior. Post-processing and data analysis are conducted using C++ and Python for visualization and data manipulation capabilities.

Learn more
A Simple Mesh Free Model for Static Analysis of Smart Composite Beams

Research has focused on Piezoelectric laminated beams for static and dynamic behaviors, including bending, vibration, buckling, and control. This study introduces a novel Element-Free Galerkin (EFG) method for simulating piezoelectric laminated beams.
